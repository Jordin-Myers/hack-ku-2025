🔐 DeFi & Payments Safety Checklist

1. Validate All Contract Addresses

Always confirm that the contract address you're interacting with is official. Use trusted sources like verified DeFi dashboards, project websites, or blockchain explorers.

  • ✅ Check the address on the project's official site
  • ✅ Compare with listings on CoinGecko, Etherscan, or XRPL Explorer
  • ✅ Avoid shortened or obfuscated links

2. Protect Your Seed Phrase & Private Keys

Your seed phrase is the master key to your wallet. If someone has it, they have full access to your funds.

  • ✅ Never enter your seed phrase into a website
  • ✅ Store it offline in a secure location
  • ✅ Use encrypted backups and avoid taking screenshots

3. Use Hardware Wallets for Significant Funds

Hardware wallets isolate your private key from the internet, drastically reducing the risk of hacks or malware theft.

  • ✅ Only buy wallets from official vendors
  • ✅ Confirm transactions on the device screen
  • ✅ Avoid using hardware wallets on public computers

4. Confirm Audit & Security Reviews

Many DeFi platforms undergo smart contract audits to ensure security. Only use platforms with reputable audits.

  • ✅ Check for third-party audit reports (e.g., Certik, Trail of Bits)
  • ✅ Read user feedback on Twitter or Reddit
  • ✅ Avoid new projects with anonymous teams and no audits

5. Be Skeptical of Airdrops, Giveaways, and Promises of Free Money

Scams often use urgency and rewards to trick users into signing malicious transactions or giving up credentials.

  • ✅ Don’t connect your wallet to unknown dApps
  • ✅ Never pay to claim an airdrop
  • ✅ Double-check project legitimacy on social media and forums
